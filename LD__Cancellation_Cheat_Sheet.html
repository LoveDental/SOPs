Cancellation Cheat Sheet

4 STEPS: Acknowledge, Empathize, Pivot to solve, Anchor value
GOAL: Save same-day or same-week. Book on the call.

⚠ The #1 Rule

Never say "OK, no problem, let's reschedule" the moment they want to cancel. That sentence costs us money every time. Always try to save today or this week first.

The Save Line

"Before we reschedule out, is there any way we can make today work? I'd hate for you to wait three weeks when we have you on the schedule right now."

📱 If a patient TEXTS to cancel: we CALL

A text gives them all the control and zero friction. Every save tool we have (warmth, concern, the doctor check, the Make Your Day Fund) only works on the phone. The text is the signal. The call is the save. Never negotiate the cancellation over text.

1
Text comes in. Don't reply to negotiate.
2
Send the heads-up text so the call is expected.
3
Call within 5 min while they're still holding the phone.
4
No answer? Voicemail + warm bridge text.
5
Wait a bit, try once more. Then graceful close.

The exception: if they clearly say "stop contacting me" or it's a real family emergency, honor it and note the file. We pursue the ordinary "I need to cancel," never someone setting a real boundary.

Bridge Texts: get them on the phone

1 · The heads-up (send the instant you see the cancel text, before you dial)

"Hi [First], it's [Name] at Love Dental. So sorry to hear you need to make a change! Going to give you a quick call in the next couple minutes so we can take care of you. 😊"

2 · Missed you, warm (you called, no answer)

"Hi [First], [Name] at Love Dental. Just tried you, no worries I missed you! I wanted to check in and see what's going on so we can find a time that works better for you. When's a good moment to catch you?"

3 · The genuine check-in (second attempt, or if they ignore #2)

"Hi [First], just thinking of you. Everything okay? Give me a quick call when you have a sec, I'd love to get you taken care of and not have you waiting weeks. 💙"

4 · The graceful close (gone quiet after a couple tries, leave the door open)

"No problem at all, [First]! I know life gets busy. Whenever you're ready, we're here and we'd love to see you. Just text or call and we'll get you right back on the schedule. 😊"

Top 10 Objections

1 · "I'm sick"
"I'm so sorry you're not well. Is it contagious or more like allergies? I have an opening [day this week or day next week] which gives you a few days to bounce back and you're also not having to wait weeks to get back in."
Goal: rebook within 7 days, not next month.
2 · "Car trouble / flat tire"
"That's the worst! Tell you what, I'm going to do something especially for you. Each of us on the team has a little leeway to take care of our patients and make someone's day, and I'd love to use mine to help you out. If you grab an Uber or Lyft, we'll put it toward today's visit, so you're saving the ride and the cancellation fee. What do you think, want me to text you the address?"
Credit up to $50. They bring the receipt at checkout. Warm, not transactional.
3 · "Childcare fell through"
"Don't you hate when that happens! Good news is, we love kids here. We have Disney, Netflix and toys for the kiddos. Instead of paying a sitter another day, bring them with you. Let's save you that babysitter money."
Frame: "save the babysitter money" = a win, not an inconvenience.
4 · "Work / boss needs me"
"Work doesn't always cooperate. Is this a one-time thing, or is your schedule usually inflexible with work? We've got 8 AM and some late-afternoon slots, let's book [day this week/next] before both our schedules fill up."
Book NOW on the call. Never "I'll call you back."
5 · "Can't afford it right now"
"I appreciate you being honest, let's figure it out together. Let's get this on monthly payments, we can get you approved in ~60 seconds and most patients qualify for 0% interest. Your insurance is awesome and covers $[X], so we just need to make $[Y] work. Want me to send the Cherry link right now?"
The magic move: send the link on the call.
6 · "I forgot / not expecting it"
"No worries! Good news, you're still on the schedule. If you can be here in 30-45 min we can still see you. If not, I can get you in [later today/tomorrow/this week] or [later today/tomorrow/this week]?"
Offer same-day FIRST.
7 · "I'll call you back"
"Yes, definitely check to see if that'll work. Tell you what, let's pencil something in now and you can adjust if needed. We're booking 2-3 weeks out. What does next Tuesday at XXX look like?"
80% who say "call back" never do. Pencil it in.
8 · "Don't feel like it today"
"Some days are like that. Is it the appointment you're not looking forward to, or just a rough day? [Anxiety:] It's quick and our team is gentle. [Bad day:] Once you're here it's often not as bad as the morning makes it feel."
Usually anxiety in disguise. Soft nudge, not pressure.
9 · "Family emergency"
"Oh my goodness, I'm so sorry. Please take care of your family, don't worry about anything on our end. When things settle, give us a call. I'll check in with you in about a week, okay?"
Don't push. But commit to a 1-week follow-up call.
10 · "Life is crazy, need to reschedule"
"I hear you. Let's get you on the books further out so it's there when things calm down. 3-4 weeks out, day of week, morning or afternoon? We'll text a few days before so you can adjust."
Take the win. Keep them booked, even if further out.

ASAP List Texts: confident, never desperate

Rules: Never say "we just had a cancellation." Use specific times ("today at 2:30" beats "this afternoon"). Keep it 2-3 lines like a friend texting. Make YES/NO easy. Don't over-explain.
1 · Same-day opening (general)

"Hi [First], it's [Name] at Love Dental. We have an opening today at 2:30 if you'd like to come in earlier than your scheduled appointment. Reply YES if you can make it and I'll get you set up. 😊"

2 · Patient booked further out

"Hi [First]! [Name] at Love Dental. Your appointment is on [date], but we just had a spot open today at [time] / tomorrow at [time]. Want me to move you up? Reply YES or NO, no worries either way."

3 · Unscheduled treatment patient

"Hey [First], it's [Name] at Love Dental. I know you've been meaning to get back in for [the crown on #14 / your filling]. I have an opening today at 3:00 if you'd like to take care of it. Reply YES and I'll lock it in."

4 · Mass text to fill a gap (day-of)

"Hi [First], Love Dental here! We have a same-day opening today at [time]. First to confirm gets the spot. Reply YES if you can make it!"

5 · Hygiene recall (overdue)

"Hi [First]! [Name] at Love Dental. Looks like it's been a few months since your last cleaning. We have openings this week: Wed at 9 AM or Fri at 2 PM. Which works better? 😊"

6 · The "I thought of you" recovery

"Hi [First], [Name] at Love Dental. A spot just opened tomorrow at [time]. I thought of you since you'd mentioned wanting to come in sooner. Want it? Reply YES and it's yours."

Two Lines to Memorize

The Save Line

"Before we reschedule out, can I ask, is there any way we can make today work? I'd really hate for you to wait three weeks when we have you on the schedule right now."

The Policy Reminder (use sparingly)

"Friendly reminder, per our attendance policy, since we're inside 48 hours there's a cancellation fee. I have some leeway if you can make it in the next day or two. I'd love to keep your appointment and avoid any fees."

💰 Cancellation Fee: the rule

First timeAlways waive it. "We'll take care of that this time, we really value you." Goodwill move.
Repeat offenderFee gets real ($50 to $75), or move them to same-day-only so they can't hold a slot they'll waste. Get a card on file going forward.
Real emergencyNo fee, ever. Concern first, warm follow-up.

The fee is a value signal, not a revenue plan. The $150 in the save script is theater we waive; $50 to $75 is what we'd actually charge, and only the repeat tier. Consistency beats the amount. Never punish a good patient for one bad day.

🚗 Uber Reimbursement (car trouble): Patient takes an Uber, screenshots/brings the receipt, we credit up to $50 toward today's visit. Frame: "Tell you what, I'm going to do something especially for you..." Warm and special, not transactional. A $20-30 ride saves a $200-800 appointment, and they tell their friends.
