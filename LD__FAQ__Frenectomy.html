Frenectomy — Front Desk FAQ
Common patient calls after a frenectomy, and how to answer them.
For front desk use. These are quick answers for common calls. The colored tag on each answer is a guide: green means reassure, no visit usually needed; amber means bring them in if it does not resolve; red means schedule them. When in doubt, or if the patient is anxious, it is always fine to bring them in.
There is a white or yellow film on it.e.g. “The area looks white/yellow.”

Reassure them, a white or yellow film over a frenectomy site is normal healing tissue, not infection or pus. Tell them not to pick at it.

Reassure, no visit needed
Do I really have to do the stretches?e.g. “Are the stretches necessary?”

Yes, this is the most important part. The gentle stretches keep the area from reattaching as it heals, which is what makes the procedure hold. Encourage them to do them as directed, with clean hands.

Reassure + coach, no visit needed
It hurts / is swollen.e.g. “It is sore.”

Soreness for a few days is normal. Pain relief on schedule, soft cool foods, salt water rinses. If it worsens after day 3 or there is fever, schedule them.

Bring in if worsening / fever
It looks like it is closing back up.e.g. “It seems to be reattaching.”

If it looks like it is reattaching despite the stretches, schedule them so the doctor can check it. Reinforce keeping up the stretches in the meantime.

Bring them in
Can I use something to help it heal?e.g. “Anything to help it heal?”

Vitamin E (over the counter or a broken gel capsule on the area) and StellaLife gel can soothe and help healing. Comfort aids alongside the stretches and keeping it clean.

Reassure + coach, no visit needed
