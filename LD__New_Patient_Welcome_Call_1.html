New Patient Welcome Call

Phone Script & Training Guide

Purpose of This Call

This is NOT a confirmation call. The patient has an appointment. We are not asking them to confirm it — we are welcoming them and building confidence that they are coming to the right place. Every word should communicate warmth, readiness, and that we are genuinely looking forward to them.

The One-Sentence Mindset"I am calling to make them feel like a VIP before they walk through the door — not to ask them something."

The Do vs. Don't Word List

Train yourself on these word swaps first. The wrong words turn a welcome call into a confirmation call.

✓ Say This

  • "We're really looking forward to meeting you"
  • "We have everything ready for you"
  • "We just wanted to reach out and welcome you"
  • "Our team is excited to take great care of you"
  • "Please don't hesitate to reach out if anything comes up"
  • "We want to make sure your first visit is as smooth as possible"
  • "We'll take care of you"

✗ Avoid This

  • "Are you still coming in? / Can you confirm?"
  • "I'm calling to remind you about your appointment"
  • "We just need to confirm your appointment"
  • "Make sure you don't forget"
  • "We need you to call us back to confirm"
  • "Is there any chance you need to reschedule?"
  • "Will you be able to make it?"

Phone Script — Patient Answers

Read this naturally — don't sound scripted. The goal is warm and confident, not robotic.

Opening  — first 5 seconds matter most
Open
"Hi, may I speak with [Patient First Name]?"
They answer
"Hi [NAME]! This is Heather calling from Love Dental, am I interrupting?"
Brief answer
[Respond genuinely — 1 sentence max.] "Wonderful! I'm calling because…"
The Welcome  — core of the call
Welcome
"I want to personally reach out and welcome you to Love Dental. We're really looking forward to having you in on [Day] at [Time]."
Readiness
"Our team has everything ready for you [NAME], and I want to make sure your first visit with us is as smooth and comfortable as possible. Is there anything you would like our Doctor or our team to know beforehand?"
Open Door
"If you have any questions before you come in — whether it's about parking, what to bring, anything at all — please don't hesitate to give us a call. We're happy to help."
Close  — warm and brief
Close
"Other than that, we just wanted to say we're all ready for you and we're excited to meet you and take great care of you. We'll see you [Day]!"
Sign Off
"Have a wonderful day/evening, [NAME]. We'll see you soon!"

When There Is No Answer

Voicemail Script  — leave only if the mailbox is clearly active. Under 30 seconds. Warm, not urgent.
"Hi [Name], this is Heather calling from Love Dental. I just wanted to personally reach out and welcome you — we're really looking forward to having you in on [Day] at [Time]. Our team has everything ready for you, and I want to make sure your visit is smooth and comfortable. If you have any questions before then, please don't hesitate to give us a call at 623-270-7420. We can't wait to meet you. Have a great day!"
Text Script  — send ONLY if no voicemail was left or the mailbox was full. Do not double up.
"Hi [NAME]! This is Heather from Love Dental. I'm reaching out to welcome you ahead of your appointment on [Day] at [Time]. 😊 Our team has everything ready for you and we're really looking forward to meeting you. If you have any questions before you come in, feel free to reply here or give us a call at 623-270-7420. See you soon!"

Notes on the text:

  • Keep the emoji to one — it warms the tone without looking unprofessional.
  • No questions. No "please confirm." No "let us know if you can make it."
  • If they reply with a cancellation or rescheduling request, hand that off per normal scheduling protocol. (Refer to cancellation scripts.)

Call Timing & Logistics

When to CallGuidelines
New Patients2 days before the appointment. Not same-day unless booked last-minute.
Emergency PatientsWithin 1 hour of booking. Reassurance matters most for this group — they're already in pain or anxious. Lead with "we're ready for you."
Best Call Times10 AM–12 PM or 4–6 PM. Avoid the typical lunch hour (12–1) unless necessary.
If No AnswerLeave voicemail OR send text — not both. Log the attempt in Open Dental.
Call Duration Target60–90 seconds. This is a welcome call, not a conversation. Be warm, be brief, get off.

Kal's Coaching Note — Read This Once a Week

The welcome call is the first impression after the patient says yes. The automated system got them to book. YOU are what makes them feel like they made the right choice. One warm, genuine phone call is worth more than 10 reminder texts. The patient should hang up thinking "these people actually care" — not "okay, I guess my appointment is confirmed." If they cancel AFTER this call, at least they cancelled as a warm patient, not a cold one. The no-show rate on patients who received a genuine welcome call is significantly lower. Once that relationship is formed, it is much easier to reconnect with them later.
