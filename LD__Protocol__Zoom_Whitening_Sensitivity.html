Zoom Whitening Sensitivity Protocol
Clinical & front-desk guide for minimizing in-chair sensitivity

Sensitivity during Philips Zoom is driven by the peroxide reaching the pulp through open dentinal tubules. The fix is to calm the nerve before the appointment and to set expectations so patients do not panic-stop mid-cycle. Follow this for every Zoom case.

Patient Selection & Screening

At the consult, flag higher-risk patients so we can prep them harder or steer to take-home trays:

  • Existing cold or pressure sensitivity
  • Exposed root surfaces or gum recession
  • Recent restorations or visible cracks
  • Enamel erosion or acid-wear history

1 to 2 Weeks Before (Highest Impact)

  • Patient brushes twice daily with a sensitivity toothpaste (Sensodyne) starting one to two weeks out.
  • This is the single biggest lever. Day-of desensitizer alone is not enough runway.
  • Front desk hands out the instruction at the time of booking and notes it in the chart.

Day Of, Before We Start

  • If the patient can take it, 600 to 800 mg Tylenol or ibuprofen about one hour before blunts the pulpal response before it starts.
  • Confirm tight, dry isolation. Leaking dam or gel migration onto soft tissue reads as "sensitivity" and is what drives most stop requests.
  • Pre-load the teeth: apply desensitizer or fluoride varnish before the first cycle, not only after.

During Treatment

  • Apply the Relief ACP gel from the kit between cycles as directed.
  • For sensitivity-prone patients, run 3 cycles instead of 4. The shade gain on cycle 4 is small and that is where the zingers spike. We protect the result and save chair time.
  • Keep cheeks, lips, and gums fully isolated the whole time.

Say This At Seat-In (prevents panic stops)

"You may feel some quick sharp zings during this. That is completely normal, it is just the teeth reacting, and it fades fast. If one is too much, raise your hand and we pause the lamp for a moment. We have got you the whole way through."

If A Patient Wants To Stop Mid-Cycle

"Totally okay. Let's pause right here and let it settle. These zings come and go, and they ease off once we add the gel. Take a breath, and we will go again when you are ready."

Then reapply gel, give 60 to 90 seconds, and resume. Most stops are surprise, not unbearable pain.

Hard Rules

  • Never push through a patient who is in real, escalating pain. Stop, reassess, and consider ending at the cycles already completed.
  • Do not skip the pre-treatment toothpaste step just because the schedule is tight. It is the whole protocol.
